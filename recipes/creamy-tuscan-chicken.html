Ultimate Creamy Tuscan Chicken

Instructions

Season the chicken cutlets on both sides with salt, pepper, and Italian seasoning. Ensure they are dry before seasoning for a better sear.

Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the chicken and sear for 5-7 minutes per side until golden brown and cooked through. Remove chicken and set aside on a plate.

In the same pan, reduce heat to medium. Add the garlic and sun-dried tomatoes. Sauté for 1-2 minutes until the garlic is fragrant, but not burnt.

Pour in the heavy cream and red pepper flakes. Bring to a light simmer and cook for 2 minutes, allowing the sauce to thicken slightly and pick up the golden bits from the bottom of the pan.

Add the spinach and Parmesan cheese. Stir until the spinach is wilted and the cheese has melted into a smooth, velvety sauce.

Return the chicken to the skillet. Spoon the sauce over the chicken and let it heat through for 1 more minute. Garnish with fresh parsley if desired and serve immediately.

You Must Know

Don't waste the flavor! The "fond" (the brown bits stuck to the pan after cooking the chicken) is culinary gold. When you pour in the heavy cream, use a wooden spoon to scrape those bits up. They will dissolve into the sauce, giving it a deep, savory flavor that you can't get any other way.

Storage Tips

Store leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Because of the heavy cream, the sauce may thicken or "separate" slightly when cold. To reheat, place in a skillet over low heat and add a splash of milk or water to bring the sauce back to its creamy consistency. Avoid reheating on high heat as it can cause the cream to break.

We do not recommend freezing this dish as the cream-based sauce can become grainy and lose its texture once thawed.

Ingredient Substitutions

  • Chicken: Shrimp, salmon, or even pork chops work beautifully with this sauce. For a vegetarian option, use thick slices of cauliflower or roasted chickpeas.
  • Heavy Cream: For a lighter version, use half-and-half or whole milk mixed with a teaspoon of cornstarch. For dairy-free, use full-fat canned coconut milk.
  • Spinach: Kale or Swiss chard can be used, though they will need an extra minute or two to wilt.
  • Sun-dried Tomatoes: If you don't have them, use cherry tomatoes halved and sautéed until they burst.
  • Parmesan: Pecorino Romano or Asiago are excellent substitutes that add a similar salty bite.

Serving Suggestions

This Creamy Tuscan Chicken is incredibly versatile. Here are my favorite ways to serve it:

  • Over a bed of al dente linguine or fettuccine pasta
  • With a side of garlic mashed potatoes to soak up the sauce
  • Alongside crusty Italian bread for dipping
  • For a low-carb meal, serve over zoodles (zucchini noodles) or steamed cauliflower
  • With a simple green salad dressed in a light vinaigrette to cut through the richness

Cultural and Historical Context

While often referred to as "Tuscan Chicken" in the United States, this dish is a prime example of Italian-American fusion rather than a strictly traditional recipe from the Tuscany region. It draws inspiration from the Italian love for simple, fresh ingredients like garlic, spinach, and sun-dried tomatoes.

The "Tuscan" name became a popular culinary trend in the late 20th century, representing a style of cooking that is rustic, hearty, and celebratory of Mediterranean flavors. It captures the essence of a warm, communal Italian meal, designed to be enjoyed slowly with family and friends.

Freezer Meal Conversion

While we don't recommend freezing the final sauce, you can "prep-freeze" this meal:

  • Place raw chicken cutlets in a freezer bag with the Italian seasoning and olive oil.
  • In a separate smaller bag, place the chopped sun-dried tomatoes and minced garlic.
  • Freeze both bags for up to 3 months.
  • To cook: Thaw overnight, then follow the instructions starting at Step 02. This saves time on prep while ensuring the cream sauce is fresh and perfectly textured.

Pro Tips

  • Use chicken cutlets or pound your chicken breasts to an even thickness. This ensures they cook quickly and uniformly without drying out.
  • Freshly grated Parmesan is key. Pre-shredded cheese is often coated in starch, which can make your sauce gritty instead of smooth.
  • Pat the chicken dry with a paper towel before seasoning. Any moisture on the surface will cause the chicken to steam instead of getting that beautiful golden crust.
  • If the sauce becomes too thick, simply add a tablespoon of pasta water or chicken broth to loosen it up.
