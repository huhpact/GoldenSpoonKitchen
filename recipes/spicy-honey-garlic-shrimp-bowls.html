Instructions

In a small bowl, whisk together the honey, soy sauce, sriracha, minced garlic, and grated ginger until well combined. Set aside.

Heat the sesame oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Ensure the pan is hot before adding the shrimp.

Add the shrimp to the skillet in a single layer. Cook for 2 minutes on each side until they turn pink and opaque. Avoid overcrowding the pan.

Pour the sauce mixture over the shrimp in the skillet. Toss to coat evenly and simmer for 1-2 minutes until the sauce bubbles and thickens into a glossy glaze.

Prepare the bowls by dividing the cooked rice and steamed broccoli. Top with the glazed shrimp and drizzle any remaining sauce from the pan over the bowls.

Garnish with sliced green onions and sesame seeds. Serve immediately while hot and enjoy!

You Must Know

Do not overcook the shrimp! Shrimp cook extremely fast and will become rubbery if left on the heat too long. As soon as they turn pink and form a "C" shape, they are done. If they curl into a tight "O" shape, they are likely overcooked. Add the sauce quickly to glaze them without toughening the meat.

Storage Tips

Store leftover shrimp and rice in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. This recipe is excellent for meal prep as the flavors continue to meld. To reheat, microwave for 60-90 seconds or toss quickly in a hot skillet with a splash of water to loosen the glaze.

We do not recommend freezing the fully assembled bowls as the broccoli and rice can change texture, but you can freeze the marinated raw shrimp for up to 2 months.

Ingredient Substitutions

  • Shrimp: Substitute with bite-sized chicken breast, salmon cubes, or extra-firm tofu for different protein options.
  • Honey: Agave nectar or maple syrup work well as vegan-friendly sweeteners.
  • Rice: Use quinoa, cauliflower rice, or rice noodles for a lower-carb or grain-free alternative.
  • Sriracha: If you don't like heat, use sweet chili sauce or simply omit it. For more heat, add red chili flakes.
  • Soy Sauce: Use tamari or coconut aminos for a gluten-free version of this dish.

Serving Suggestions

Elevate your shrimp bowls with these tasty additions:

  • Add a side of quick-pickled cucumbers for a refreshing crunch
  • Top with fresh avocado slices for healthy fats and creaminess
  • Squeeze fresh lime juice over the top to brighten the flavors
  • Stir in some shredded carrots or snap peas for extra veggie power
  • Drizzle with a little bit of spicy mayo if you want extra indulgence

Cultural and Historical Context

This dish is a modern take on the "Honey Garlic" flavor profile, which has roots in various East Asian culinary traditions but has become a global favorite in "Fusion" cooking. The combination of sweet honey and savory soy sauce creates a classic Umami balance that is particularly popular in American-Chinese and Canadian-Chinese cuisine.

Shrimp bowls represent the modern trend toward "deconstructed" meals, focusing on fresh, whole ingredients arranged visually for both health and aesthetic appeal. It captures the essence of fast, high-quality home cooking that mimics the experience of a trendy bistro.

Freezer Meal Conversion

You can turn this into a freezer-ready meal with these steps:

  • Place the raw shrimp and the sauce mixture into a gallon-sized freezer bag
  • Squeeze out the air and seal tightly
  • Freeze for up to 2 months
  • When ready to eat, thaw the bag in the refrigerator overnight
  • Cook as directed in Step 03 and 04. Cook the rice and broccoli fresh for the best texture

Pro Tips

  • Pat the shrimp dry with paper towels before cooking to ensure a good sear
  • Use fresh ginger and garlic instead of powders—the aromatic difference is massive
  • If the sauce thickens too much, add a teaspoon of water to reach your desired consistency
  • Toast the sesame seeds in a dry pan for 1 minute before garnishing to release their oils
  • Rinse your jasmine rice under cold water until it runs clear for the fluffiest results
