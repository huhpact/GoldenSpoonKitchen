The Traditional Meat Lasagna

Why You'll Love This Recipe

  • No more "lasagna soup." These layers stay stacked on your plate.
  • Mixing beef and Italian sausage adds a savory depth that beef alone can't touch.
  • The egg-bound ricotta creates a rich, velvety center that doesn't run.
  • We use a double-cheese topping strategy for those legendary golden, crispy edges.

Instructions

In a large pot or Dutch oven, brown the beef, sausage, and onion over medium-high heat. Break the meat into small crumbles.

Drain the excess grease before moving to the next step to prevent an oily lasagna.

Stir in the garlic and tomato paste and cook for 2 minutes. Add the crushed tomatoes and all seasonings. Lower the heat and simmer for 20–30 minutes until thick and rich.

Cook the noodles in salted water for 2 minutes less than the "al dente" time on the box. Drain and lay them flat on parchment paper.

In a medium bowl, whisk the ricotta, egg, and the first half-cup of Parmesan until fully combined.

Spread 1 cup of meat sauce on the bottom of a 9x13 inch baking dish to prevent sticking.

Lay down 3–4 noodles. Spread 1/3 of the ricotta mixture over the noodles, then top with 1/4 of the mozzarella and a generous ladle of meat sauce.

Do this two more times (Noodles -> Ricotta -> Mozzarella -> Sauce).

Place the final noodles on top, spread the remaining sauce, and cover with the rest of the mozzarella and Parmesan.

Cover with oiled foil and bake at 375°F (190°C) for 25 minutes. Remove foil and bake for another 20 minutes until bubbly and golden.

Remove from the oven and let it sit for 15 minutes before slicing.

You Must Know

The 15-minute rest is the most important part of the recipe. If you cut it immediately, the layers will collapse and the juices will run. Also, avoid "Fresh Mozzarella" (the kind in liquid); it will make your lasagna watery. Use the low-moisture block variety.

Storage Tips

Fridge: This dish is even better on day two. Cover tightly; it lasts 4 days.

Freezer: Freeze individual baked slices in airtight containers for up to 3 months.

Reheating: Reheat in the oven at 350°F (175°C) covered in foil with a splash of water in the dish to keep it from drying out.

Ingredient Substitutions

  • Meatless: Substitute meat with 2 lbs of sautéed mushrooms and zucchini (squeeze all water out of the zucchini first).
  • No Ricotta: Use Cottage Cheese, but blend it in a food processor until smooth before adding the egg.
  • Gluten-Free: Use GF-certified lasagna noodles; they hold up surprisingly well in this heavy sauce.

Serving Suggestions

Pair with a crisp Caesar salad and thick, buttery garlic bread. For wine, choose a high-tannin red like a Barolo or a classic Chianti Classico.

Success Stories

Pro Tips

  • Fresh Grating: Always grate your mozzarella from a block. Pre-shredded cheese is coated in starch that prevents a perfect melt.
  • Spray the Foil: Always spray the underside of your aluminum foil with non-stick spray so the top layer of cheese doesn't stick to it when you uncover the dish.
