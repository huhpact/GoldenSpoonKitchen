Homemade Margherita Pizza

A true Neapolitan classic with mozzarella and basil

Back to Recipes
Prep Time20 minutes
Cook Time15 minutes
Total Time35 minutes
Servings2 pizzas
DifficultyEasy

Ingredients

  • 500g pizza dough (homemade or store-bought)
  • 200g tomato sauce (San Marzano if possible)
  • 250g fresh mozzarella, sliced
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • 2 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

Prepare the dough

Preheat oven to 250°C (480°F). Stretch or roll out pizza dough on a floured surface into 2 medium bases.

Spread the sauce

Spread a thin layer of tomato sauce evenly over each base, leaving a 1–2 cm border for the crust.

Top with mozzarella

Arrange mozzarella slices evenly and add a few basil leaves. Drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle with a pinch of salt.

Bake

Bake on a preheated pizza stone or tray for 10–15 minutes until the crust is blistered and cheese is bubbling.

Finish & serve

Remove from oven, add fresh basil and a drizzle of olive oil. Slice and serve immediately.

Nutrition Information

Per Serving: Approximately 600 calories

Good source of protein and calcium from mozzarella; balanced energy from the crust.

Chef's Tips for Perfect Pizza

  • Very hot oven: The hotter, the better — it creates leopard spots on the crust like in Naples.
  • Use a pizza stone: It helps retain heat and crisps the base faster.
  • Less is more: Keep toppings minimal for the classic Margherita experience.
  • Fresh basil: Add right after baking to keep the flavor bright.

The Story Behind This Recipe

Margherita Pizza was created in Naples in 1889 in honor of Queen Margherita. The combination of tomato, mozzarella and basil represents the colors of the Italian flag and celebrates the simple excellence of Italian ingredients.
