The Ultimate Beef Chili

Robust, spicy, and satisfying—a true American comfort food tradition.

Cook Time1 hour 30 min

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 kg ground beef (80/20 lean/fat ratio)
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 1 green bell pepper, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 x 400g cans crushed tomatoes
  • 1 x 400g can kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 x 400g can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 250 ml beef broth
  • 4 tbsp chili powder
  • 1 tbsp ground cumin
  • 1 tbsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper (optional, for extra heat)
  • Salt & freshly ground black pepper to taste

Instructions

1 — Brown the beef

Heat olive oil in a large Dutch oven or heavy pot over medium-high heat. Add the ground beef and cook, breaking it up with a spoon, until fully browned. Drain off any excess fat and set the beef aside.

2 — Sauté aromatics

Add the chopped onion and bell pepper to the pot and sauté until softened (about 5–7 minutes). Add the minced garlic and cook for 1 minute until fragrant.

3 — Add and toast spices

Stir in the chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika, oregano, and cayenne pepper. Cook for 1–2 minutes, stirring constantly. This step, known as toasting, is crucial for releasing the full flavor of the spices.

4 — Simmer the chili

Return the beef to the pot. Stir in the crushed tomatoes, drained beans, and beef broth. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover partially and let it simmer for at least 1.5 hours (longer is better, up to 3 hours), stirring occasionally.

5 — Finish and serve

Remove the lid for the last 30 minutes if you want a thicker consistency. Season generously with salt and pepper. Serve hot with your favorite toppings like shredded cheddar cheese, sour cream, chopped chives, or crispy tortilla chips.

Chef's Tips for the Best Chili

  • The longer, the better: The chili’s flavor deepens significantly with a long, slow simmer. Aim for at least 90 minutes.
  • Toast the Spices: Don't skip step 3! Cooking the dry spices briefly in oil/fat activates their flavor compounds.
  • Don't skimp on chili powder: Use a high-quality chili powder blend for the best results. It is the primary flavor component.
  • Use a secret ingredient: A tablespoon of cocoa powder or a splash of espresso added during the simmer can enhance the color and depth of the beef flavor.
  • Freezing: Chili freezes wonderfully. Make a large batch and store portions for quick future meals.

The Story Behind This Recipe

Chili Con Carne (Chili with Meat) has roots deep in Texan and Southwestern U.S. history, often debated between San Antonio and early frontier cooking. While versions without beans (like "Texas Chili") are fierce competitors, the version with beans and tomatoes became the defining comfort food across the U.S. and beyond. It’s a staple for football game days, cook-offs, and chilly evenings, evolving into a cultural icon that speaks to hearty, satisfying American cuisine.
