Authentic Buffalo Chicken Wings

The ultimate game day snack: crispy, spicy, and completely addictive.

Prep Time15 minutes
Cook Time45 minutes
Total Time1 hour
Servings4 people
DifficultyEasy

Ingredients

  • 1.5 kg chicken wings, tips removed, split into flats and drumettes
  • 1 tbsp aluminum-free baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 120 ml Frank's RedHot Original Cayenne Pepper Sauce
  • 60 g unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 tbsp distilled white vinegar
  • 1/4 tsp Worcestershire sauce
  • Celery sticks and Blue Cheese dressing (for serving)

Instructions

Prepare the Wings

Preheat your oven to 220°C (425°F). Line a large baking sheet with foil and place a wire rack on top. Pat the chicken wings extremely dry with paper towels — this is the key to crispiness.

The Baking Powder Secret

In a large bowl, toss the wings with baking powder and salt until evenly coated. The baking powder helps break down the proteins in the skin, creating a textured, crispy surface similar to deep-frying.

Bake for Crunch

Arrange the wings on the wire rack in a single layer. Bake for 45–50 minutes, flipping them halfway through, until they are deep golden brown and very crispy to the touch.

Mix the Buffalo Sauce

While the wings bake, whisk together the hot sauce, melted butter, vinegar, and Worcestershire sauce in a large bowl. This creates that classic, velvety restaurant-style buffalo glaze.

Toss and Serve

As soon as the wings come out of the oven, toss them in the buffalo sauce until completely coated. Serve immediately with blue cheese dressing and crunchy celery sticks.

Chef's Tips for Best Buffalo Wings

  • Don't skip the wire rack: Elevating the wings allows hot air to circulate underneath, ensuring they don't get soggy in their own fat.
  • Aluminum-Free Baking Powder: Make sure your baking powder is aluminum-free to avoid any metallic aftertaste.
  • Frank's is Non-Negotiable: Authentic Buffalo sauce must use Frank's RedHot for that specific vinegar-heavy tang.
  • The Dry-Brine: If you have time, let the coated wings sit in the fridge uncovered for 3 hours before baking for even more crunch.

The Legend of the Wing

Invented at the Anchor Bar in Buffalo, New York, in 1964, these wings were originally a late-night snack for hungry friends. Teressa Bellissimo took leftover chicken wings, deep-fried them, and tossed them in a mixture of hot sauce and butter. Today, they are the undisputed king of American bar food and an essential part of US sports culture.
