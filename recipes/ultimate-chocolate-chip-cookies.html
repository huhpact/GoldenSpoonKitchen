The Ultimate Brown Butter Chocolate Chip Cookies

This is not your average back-of-the-bag recipe. By browning the butter and allowing the dough to age in the refrigerator, we create a cookie with a deep, butterscotch-like complexity and the perfect contrast between a gooey center and shattered-glass crispy edges.

Why You'll Love This Recipe

  • Liquid gold butter adds notes of toasted hazelnuts and caramel.
  • Hand-chopped chocolate creates "pools" of molten cocoa and varying textures.
  • Aging the dough hydrates the flour and concentrates the flavor—patience is the key ingredient.
  • A higher ratio of brown sugar ensures a soft, chewy "bend" to every cookie.
  • The addition of flaky sea salt on top cuts through the sugar for a sophisticated finish.

Instructions

Place butter in a light-colored saucepan. Melt over medium heat. Once it melts, it will crackle (water evaporating) and then foam. Watch closely! When you see brown specks at the bottom and smell a nutty aroma, remove it immediately. Scrape all those brown bits into a bowl—that's the flavor.

Add both sugars to the warm butter. Whisk by hand for about 2 minutes. The sugar won't fully dissolve, but the mixture should look like wet sand.

Add the egg, the extra yolk (for richness), and the vanilla. Whisk intensely for 1-2 minutes until the mixture turns a shade lighter and looks smooth. This creates the "crackly" top.

Sift in the flour, baking soda, baking powder, and salt. Use a spatula to fold gently. Stop the moment you see no more dry flour. Overmixing develops gluten and makes cookies tough!

Fold in the chopped chocolate. Ensure you include the "chocolate dust" from the cutting board—it tints the dough beautifully.

Cover the dough tightly and chill in the fridge for at least 24 hours. This allows enzymes to break down starches into simple sugars, creating a much deeper flavor.

Preheat oven to 175°C (350°F). Scoop 50g balls (about 3 tbsp) and place them far apart on a lined tray. Bake for 10-12 minutes. The edges should be golden brown, but the centers should still look slightly underbaked.

Sprinkle with flaky salt while hot. Let them cool on the pan for 10 minutes to set.

You Must Know

Precision is everything in baking. Use a digital scale. Measuring flour by the cup can result in up to 30% more flour than needed, leading to dry, cakey cookies. Also, the extra egg yolk is the difference between a "good" cookie and a "professionally chewy" cookie.

Storage Tips

Store in an airtight container for up to 3 days (add a slice of bread to the container to keep them soft).

Scoop the dough into balls and freeze. Bake directly from frozen at 160°C (320°F) for 14-15 minutes.

10 seconds in the microwave brings back the "molten" chocolate state perfectly.

Ingredient Substitutions

  • Butter: You can use vegan butter, but you cannot brown it the same way.
  • Dark Brown Sugar: If you only have light brown, add 1 tsp of molasses.
  • Chocolate: Milk chocolate works, but it will be much sweeter. A mix of 50/50 dark and milk is a crowd favorite.

Serving Suggestions

This is a rich, heavy dish that deserves to be the star of the show. Here is how to complete the experience:

  • Best served warm with a cold glass of whole milk.
  • Use two cookies to make the ultimate vanilla bean ice cream sandwich.
  • Top with a drizzle of salted caramel for an over-the-top dessert.

Pro Tips

  • The "Pan Bang" Technique: 2 minutes before the cookies are done, lift the tray and drop it flat on the oven rack. This ripples the edges and creates a denser, chewier center.
  • The Cookie Ring: If your cookies come out uneven, place a large circular cookie cutter over the hot cookie and move it in a circular motion to "scoot" the cookie into a perfect circle.
  • Vanilla Paste: Use vanilla bean paste instead of extract to see those beautiful tiny black vanilla specks in the dough.
