The Ultimate Chocolate Chip Cookies

Classic American perfection—crispy edges and pools of chocolate.

Cook Time10 minutes
Total Time25 minutes
Yield24 Cookies
DifficultyEasy

Ingredients

  • 225 g unsalted butter, softened to room temperature
  • 200 g light brown sugar, firmly packed
  • 100 g granulated white sugar
  • 2 large eggs, room temperature
  • 1 tbsp pure vanilla extract
  • 350 g all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp fine sea salt
  • 300 g semi-sweet chocolate chips (or chunks)
  • Sea salt flakes (for finishing)

Instructions

Cream the butter and sugars

In a large bowl, cream together the softened butter, brown sugar, and white sugar until the mixture is light, fluffy, and well combined. This usually takes about 2 to 3 minutes with an electric mixer.

Incorporate wet ingredients

Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. Stir in the vanilla extract until the mixture is smooth.

Mix dry ingredients

In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, and salt. Gradually add this to the butter mixture on low speed until just combined. Do not overmix, as this can make the cookies tough.

Add the chocolate

Using a spatula, gently fold in the chocolate chips or chunks until they are evenly distributed throughout the dough.

Chill and Bake

For the best flavor, chill the dough for at least 30 minutes. Preheat your oven to 175°C (350°F). Scoop rounded tablespoons of dough onto parchment-lined baking sheets, leaving space for spreading. Bake for 9–11 minutes.

Cooling

Let the cookies rest on the baking sheet for 5 minutes before transferring them to a wire rack. Sprinkle with sea salt flakes while still warm if desired.

Chef's Tips for Perfect Cookies

  • Room Temperature Eggs: These emulsify better with the butter for a smoother dough.
  • Don't Overbake: The cookies should look slightly underdone in the middle when you take them out. They will firm up as they cool.
  • Measure Flour Correctly: Use a scale or the "spoon and level" method to avoid adding too much flour.
  • Chill the Dough: Chilling for 24 hours results in a deeper, toffee-like flavor.

The Legend of the Chocolate Chip Cookie

Invented by Ruth Wakefield at the Toll House Inn in Massachusetts during the 1930s, this recipe has become the definitive American dessert. What started as an experiment became a global phenomenon. Our version focuses on the "chewy" texture that defines the modern bakery style found in New York City's best shops.
