The Famous "Marry Me" Chicken

This is the modern gold standard for a weeknight "impressive" dinner. It’s a one-pan wonder where succulent chicken breasts are seared to perfection and then smothered in a rich, creamy, and slightly spicy sun-dried tomato sauce. It’s "modern-American" because it’s designed to be fast, photogenic, and intensely flavorful. It’s sophisticated enough for a date but easy enough for a Tuesday.

Instructions

Prep the Chicken: Slice your chicken breasts into thin cutlets and season generously with salt and pepper. Dredge them in the flour, shaking off the excess.

The Sear: Heat the oil and butter in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Sear the chicken for about 4–5 minutes per side until golden brown and cooked through. Remove to a plate.

The Aromatics: Reduce heat to medium. In the same pan, add the garlic and sauté for 1 minute.

Deglaze: Pour in the chicken broth. Use a wooden spoon to scrape all those delicious browned bits from the bottom of the pan—that’s where the flavor is!

The Creamy Base: Whisk in the heavy cream and let it come to a gentle simmer.

The "Marriage": Add the sun-dried tomatoes, parmesan, oregano, and red pepper flakes. Stir until the cheese is melted and the sauce begins to thicken (about 2–3 minutes).

Final Simmer: Return the chicken to the skillet. Spoon the creamy sauce over each piece and let it simmer for another 2 minutes to heat through.

Garnish: Top with fresh basil and serve immediately.

You Must Know

The chicken needs to be thin. Slicing it into cutlets ensures it cooks fast and stays juicy. If you use a whole thick breast, it will be dry on the outside before it's done on the inside. Also, keep the heat at a "simmer"—boiling the heavy cream too vigorously can cause the sauce to separate.

Storage Tips

This stores reasonably well in the fridge for up to 3 days. When reheating, add a small splash of milk or water to the pan to loosen the sauce back up, as the parmesan and cream will thicken significantly when cold.

Ingredient Substitutions

  • The Protein: You can substitute the chicken with large Shrimp or thick slices of Roasted Cauliflower for a vegetarian version.
  • The Cream: If you absolutely must, you can use Half & Half, but you’ll need to add a teaspoon of cornstarch to keep the sauce thick.
  • The Spice: If you like heat, double the red pepper flakes or add a teaspoon of Calabrian chili paste.

Serving Suggestions

This is a rich, heavy dish that deserves to be the star of the show. Here is how to complete the experience:

  • Classic Italian-American: Serve over a bed of al dente Linguine or Penne.
  • Low Carb: Serve over Zucchini noodles or with a side of steamed broccoli.
  • The Dip: Serve with a loaf of warm, crusty Garlic Bread to mop up every drop of that sauce.

Pro Tips

  • Tomato Oil: Don't throw away the oil from the sun-dried tomato jar! Use a tablespoon of it instead of regular olive oil for an even deeper tomato flavor.
  • Don't Overcrowd: Sear the chicken in batches if your pan is small. If they touch, they will steam instead of sear.
  • Fresh Basil: Don't skip it! The brightness of the fresh basil cuts through the richness of the cream and cheese perfectly.
