Authentic New York Style Cheesecake

The ultimate dessert: dense, velvety, and incredibly rich.

Cook Time1 hour 15m
Chill Time6 hours
Servings12 Slices
DifficultyAdvanced

Ingredients

  • 200g Graham cracker crumbs
  • 100g unsalted butter, melted
  • 900g full-fat cream cheese, room temperature
  • 200g granulated white sugar
  • 240g full-fat sour cream
  • 4 large eggs, room temperature
  • 1 tbsp pure vanilla extract
  • 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour

Instructions

Create the Foundation

Preheat your oven to 175°C (350°F). Combine graham cracker crumbs and melted butter. Press the mixture firmly into the bottom of a 9-inch springform pan. Bake for 10 minutes, then let it cool while you prepare the filling.

Cream the Filling

In a large bowl, beat the cream cheese and sugar until completely smooth and creamy. Use a low speed to avoid whipping too much air into the mixture.

Add the Aromatics

Stir in the sour cream, vanilla extract, lemon juice, and flour. These ingredients provide the signature New York tang and help the structure of the cake.

Gentle Egg Incorporation

Add the eggs one at a time. Mix on low speed just until combined. Do not overbeat, as air bubbles will cause the cheesecake to crack during baking.

The Bain-Marie (Water Bath)

Wrap the outside of the pan in several layers of heavy-duty foil. Place it in a large roasting pan and fill the roasting pan with hot water halfway up the sides. Bake at 160°C (320°F) for 75 minutes.

Cooling & Chilling

Turn off the oven and leave the door slightly ajar for one hour. This gradual cooling prevents cracks. Afterward, refrigerate for at least 6 hours or overnight before serving.

Chef's Tips for a Perfect Cheesecake

  • Room Temperature is Key: Ensure your cream cheese, eggs, and sour cream are at room temperature to prevent lumps.
  • No Peeking: Don't open the oven door during baking. The sudden change in temperature can cause the cake to collapse.
  • The Jiggle: The cheesecake is done when the edges are set but the center still has a slight jiggle.
  • Cleaning the Knife: For perfect bakery-style slices, wipe your knife clean with a hot, damp cloth between every single cut.

A New York Icon

While cheesecake has roots in ancient Greece, the "New York Style" was perfected in the early 20th century. Legend has it that Arnold Reuben, owner of the famous Turf Restaurant, was served a cheese pie and became obsessed with recreating it. His version used pure cream cheese, resulting in the dense, silky texture we love today. It has since become the definitive American dessert.
