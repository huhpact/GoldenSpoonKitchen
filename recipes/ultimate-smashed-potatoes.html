Instructions

Boil: Place potatoes in a pot of cold, heavily salted water. Bring to a boil and cook for 15–20 minutes until easily pierced by a fork.

Steam Dry: Drain completely. Let the potatoes sit in the colander for 5–10 minutes. This evaporation step is the #1 secret for achieving a crispy skin.

Smash: Preheat your oven to 220°C (425°F). Arrange potatoes on a greased baking sheet. Use the bottom of a heavy glass or a potato masher to press each one until flat but still structurally intact.

Season: Whisk oil, garlic, and herbs together. Brush or drizzle this mixture over every potato, ensuring it gets into the cracks.

Roast: Bake for 25–30 minutes, flipping halfway through if you want both sides extra crispy.

Finish: Season with flaky sea salt and fresh herbs immediately after pulling them out of the oven.

You Must Know

The "steam-dry" step is non-negotiable. If you leave moisture on the surface, the potato will steam rather than crisp up, resulting in a rubbery texture instead of a "shattered glass" crunch. Also, do not overcrowd the baking sheet; if the potatoes are touching, they will soften rather than roast.

Storage Tips

Smashed potatoes are best eaten immediately. If you have leftovers, store them in the fridge and reheat them in a hot oven or air fryer to restore the crispiness. Do not use a microwave.

Ingredient Substitutions

  • Fat: For a decadent variation, use melted butter or duck fat instead of olive oil.
  • Cheese: Dust with finely grated Parmesan in the final 5 minutes of roasting for an umami-packed crust.
  • Spice: Add a teaspoon of Calabrian chili paste or smoked paprika to the oil mixture for a modern, spicy kick.

Serving Suggestions

This is a rich, heavy dish that deserves to be the star of the show. Here is how to complete the experience:

  • The Classic: Serve alongside grilled steak or roasted chicken.
  • Gourmet Style: Top with a dollop of crème fraîche, chives, and a bit of smoked salmon.
  • Street Food Vibe: Drizzle with a spicy mayo or herb-infused aioli.

Pro Tips

  • Uniformity: Pick potatoes of similar size so they all reach tenderness at the same time.
  • Hot Surface: Preheat your baking sheet in the oven while you smash the potatoes; placing them on a hot pan gives you a head-start on the crispiness.
  • Oil Usage: Don't be afraid to be generous with the oil—it acts as a conductor for the heat to create that signature golden crust.
