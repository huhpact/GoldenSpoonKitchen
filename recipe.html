Instructions

Preheat oven to 350°F. Coat a 9x5 inch loaf pan with non-stick spray and line with parchment paper.

In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour, baking powder, and salt until well combined.

In a separate medium bowl, whisk oil, granulated sugar, sour cream, limoncello, lemon zest, eggs, lemon extract, vanilla extract, and yellow food coloring (if desired) until smooth.

Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and gently fold until just combined. Do not overmix.

Pour batter into the prepared pan and bake for 50-55 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean. Let cool in pan for 15 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack.

For the glaze: Whisk together powdered sugar and limoncello until smooth. Drizzle over the cooled cake. Let the glaze set for 10-15 minutes before slicing and serving.

You Must Know

Room temperature ingredients are crucial for this recipe! Make sure your eggs and sour cream are at room temperature before starting - this ensures a smooth, well-emulsified batter that bakes evenly. Cold ingredients can cause the batter to separate or create a dense texture.

Storage Tips

Store the limoncello cake in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days, or refrigerate for up to 5 days. The glaze may soften slightly when refrigerated, but the cake will remain moist. For best flavor, bring to room temperature before serving.

You can also freeze unglazed cake slices wrapped tightly in plastic wrap and aluminum foil for up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator and add the glaze just before serving.

Ingredient Substitutions

  • Limoncello: Can't find limoncello? Substitute with 2-3 tablespoons of fresh lemon juice mixed with 1 tablespoon vodka or simply use 3 tablespoons lemon juice.
  • Sour cream: Greek yogurt works beautifully as a 1:1 substitute and adds extra protein.
  • Canola oil: Use vegetable oil, melted coconut oil, or even melted butter for a richer flavor.
  • All-purpose flour: For a gluten-free version, use a 1:1 gluten-free flour blend.
  • Granulated sugar: You can use caster sugar or superfine sugar for an even smoother texture.

Serving Suggestions

This limoncello cake is perfect on its own, but here are some delicious ways to elevate your presentation:

  • Serve with a dollop of fresh whipped cream and fresh berries
  • Pair with a scoop of vanilla gelato or lemon sorbet
  • Add a dusting of powdered sugar and candied lemon slices
  • Serve alongside espresso or limoncello for an authentic Italian experience
  • Top with a lemon curd layer for extra citrus intensity

Cultural and Historical Context

Limoncello is a traditional Italian lemon liqueur that originated in Southern Italy, particularly along the Amalfi Coast, Sorrento, and the island of Capri. Made from lemon zest, alcohol, water, and sugar, this bright yellow liqueur has been a staple of Italian culture for over a century.

While limoncello itself is typically served as a digestif after meals, Italian bakers have long incorporated it into desserts, creating cakes that capture the essence of the Mediterranean sun. This cake represents the Italian philosophy of using simple, quality ingredients to create something truly memorable.

Freezer Meal Conversion

This cake freezes beautifully! To prepare as a freezer-friendly dessert:

  • Bake the cake completely and allow it to cool to room temperature
  • Do NOT add the glaze before freezing
  • Wrap the unglazed cake tightly in plastic wrap, then in aluminum foil
  • Label with the date and freeze for up to 3 months
  • To serve: Thaw overnight in the refrigerator, bring to room temperature, then prepare and add the fresh glaze
  • For individual servings, slice before freezing and wrap each slice separately for grab-and-go convenience

Pro Tips

  • Use fresh lemon zest for the brightest flavor - avoid pre-grated zest
  • Don't overmix the batter once you combine wet and dry ingredients; this keeps the cake tender
  • For an extra limoncello punch, poke holes in the warm cake and brush with additional limoncello before glazing
  • The cake actually tastes better the next day as the flavors have time to develop
  • Use a microplane grater for the finest lemon zest that incorporates beautifully into the batter
